10 of the most-read stories from 2025

Site: KEENESENTINEL
Published: 2025-12-22

With the new year right around the corner, it's a time for reflection.

As with every year, the Monadnock Region was home to hope and devastation, new beginnings and sudden endings.

To look back on all that's happened, here's a list of some of the most-read articles on our website from 2025:

* Brendan Bilgen of Keene traveled from Dubai to Vietnam to Norway and more in the hospitality business before returning to the Granite State to sharpen knives.

* After the state made cuts in funding to higher education, Keene State College began to eliminate staff positions and ask faculty to retire.

* New Hampshire lawmakers passed a bill that, at the end of January 2026, eliminates the yearly vehicle inspection requirement.

* In a packed courtroom in February, authorities alleged two women suffered significant abuse at a Keene home , one of whom died in the home on Island Street in October 2024.

* A new Euro-themed cafe opened in Keene at the end of October, serving up a variety of coffee and central-European baked goods.

* Two weeks to the day after he lost his arm in a workplace accident in Winchester, Steven Lawrence celebrated his 38th birthday in a Boston hospital, alive and in good spirits.

* As multiple insurers in the Granite State started to drop their Medicare Advantage plans, seniors scrambled to figure out their coverage.

* After The Sentinel's years-long court battle over the public's right to know, the N.H. Supreme Court ruled that Fall Mountain School District had to release documents, which indicated that administrators found a former longtime teacher at the Langdon high school engaged in sexually harassing behavior against female colleagues.

* A four-alarm fire on Elliot Street in Keene in November sent five firefighters to the hospital and destroyed a home where nine people lived.

* Regional pizza chain Ramunto’s announced plans for the revival of its location in Keene.
