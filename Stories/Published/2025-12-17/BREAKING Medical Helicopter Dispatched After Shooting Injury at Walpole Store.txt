Emergency Helicopter Called Following Gunshot Injury at Walpole Store

WALPOLE, N.H. Emergency services were dispatched late Wednesday morning to Jiffy Mart on Main Street due to an incident involving a gunshot wound. The call came in before 11 a.m., leading responders to request air medical transport given the serious nature of the injury.

The medical helicopter, operated by DHART, was heading to Cheshire Medical Center's helipad to facilitate the patient's transfer and provide necessary medical care. Meanwhile, updates from officials indicated that there was no immediate threat to the public from this incident.

Details regarding the victim's condition and the events leading to the shooting were not available at the time. This situation is ongoing, and further updates will be provided as information becomes available.

In other local news, a show of solidarity was observed across Cheshire County as law enforcement paid tribute to Deputy Mark Chamberlain of the Cheshire County Sheriffs Department with a convoy past his residence. Additionally, Eversource announced that New Hampshire customers could anticipate a slight reduction in electric bills starting in early 2026 due to stable supply costs and declining delivery rates. Furthermore, nearly $224,000 in state LCHIP grants will aid forest conservation in Nelson and fund the restoration of the historic National Bank building in Winchester. Lastly, holiday parking regulations will be relaxed in downtown Keene starting Thursday, providing shoppers relief from meter fees until Christmas Day, though standard parking rules will still be in effect.
