High winds cause damage, outages

Site: WCAX
Published: 2025-12-19
URL: https://www.wcax.com/2025/12/19/high-winds-cause-damage-outages/

BURLINGTON, Vt. (WCAX) - High winds tore through the region Friday causing damage and knocking out power.

At it’s peak around noon, Upwards of 22,000 customers were without power in Vermont, according to VT outages The hardest hit areas were Orleans, Caledonia, Lamoille, and Rutland Counties.

Upwards of 10,000 utility customers were without power across New Hampshire.

Video from Mount Holly showed branches covering parts of the road. Hundreds were without power in town for part of the day.

Heavy winds blew parts off the building housing Our House Bistro on the Winooski Circle. Officials say about a 24 by 16 foot section of brick veneer fell off, hit a neighboring building, and fell into the alley below. No one was hurt and officials say the building is not dangerous. Authorities spent hours on scene checking the building’s structural integrity.

Mount Washington recorded top wind gusts of up to 118 miles per hour Friday morning.

Authorities say that with winds and icing continuing into the evening, there will be hazardous travel conditions.
