Snow moving into NH ahead of Christmas

Site: WMUR
Published: 2025-12-23
URL: https://www.wmur.com/article/new-hampshire-weather-snow-122325/69848632

Cold, snowy conditions are expected across New Hampshire on Tuesday, just two days before Christmas.

A winter storm warning is in effect for most of Maine and all of Carroll County in New Hampshire. Otherwise, a winter weather advisory is in effect for the rest of New Hampshire, except for the counties that border Massachusetts.

Tuesday began cold as clouds filled in.

Snow began falling during the early afternoon and will continue into the evening.

As snowfall picks up, roads could become slick and visibility may be reduced at times. The evening commute is likely to be slow.

Most southern and far western areas in New Hampshire should see 1 to 3 inches of snow, with up to 4 inches possible in higher terrain.

Areas of the Lakes Region, White Mountains and North Country could see 3 to 6 inches.

As the storm strengthens offshore Tuesday night, heavier snow will fall toward the east in Maine. Some of the heavier snow showers could edge back into northeastern New Hampshire, resulting in an additional inch or two of snow by Wednesday morning in parts of the White Mountains.

Lingering flakes clear during the early morning of Wednesday, as it turns breezy and colder for Christmas Eve.

Parts of central and northern New Hampshire could wake up to flurries on Christmas morning, but otherwise a quiet day of weather is expected for the holiday.

Friday, during the day, will feature the best weather for post-holiday travel.

Another system could bring the chance for more light snow and wintry mix Friday night into early Saturday.

Another system is expected to approach Sunday, bringing the possibility for snow or a wintry mix.
