Four feet of snow fell in Danbury during a winter storm in New Hampshire in 2020.

Snow piled up quickly across the state over Dec. 16-17, 2020, with several communities picking up 2-3 feet of snow .

Danbury saw 48 inches of snow during that storm, the National Weather Service confirmed . It was the highest snow total in the region from the storm.

It was also the second-highest one-day snow total in the state since records have been kept. The most snow recorded in a 24-hour period in the state was 49 inches on Mount Washington.

Seven New Hampshire towns topped out with at least 40 inches of snow and 20 towns hit the 30-inch mark.
