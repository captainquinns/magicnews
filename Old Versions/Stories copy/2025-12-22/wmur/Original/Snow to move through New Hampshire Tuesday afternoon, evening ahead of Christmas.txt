Snow to move through New Hampshire Tuesday afternoon, evening ahead of Christmas

Site: WMUR
Published: 2025-12-22
URL: https://www.wmur.com/article/new-hampshire-weather-snow-122225/69837150

Cold and snowy conditions are likely in New Hampshire ahead of Christmas.

Ahead of the snow, Monday will be bright and breezy. Highs will be in the 20s, but it will feel colder with wind gusts up to 30 mph.

The winds will lighten Monday night, as temperatures drip into the teens and low 20s by Tuesday morning.

Two days before Christmas, on Tuesday, New Hampshire will see its next statewide snowfall.

It won't be a big storm, but some snow is definitely on the way for the Granite State on Tuesday.

The snow will have an impact on travel conditions, as some slippery spots are likely.

On average, expect steady light snow that will fall moderately, at times.

The timing is not locked in, but it looks as if Tuesday morning will be mostly dry. Snow is likely to move in midday or during the afternoon.

As the snow picks up, it could lead to slippery roads and low visibility, at times. The evening commute could be slow and treacherous.

All of the snow will wrap up by Daybreak on Wednesday.

Most of central and southern New Hampshire should see about 1-3 inches of snow. Areas north of the Lakes Region, from the White Mountains through the North Country, could see 3-6 inches.
